What the product contains

Two engines, one scalable product.

Auctor has to avoid becoming a consulting-heavy assessment agency. The app is designed as a self-serve platform: every customer configures, launches, pays, and improves their workforce intelligence without needing a growing services team behind them.

01

The Lab

Workers complete modular physical micro-craft challenges through phone or webcam capture. Computer vision detects actions, hesitation, recovery, iteration, and precision under constraint.

02

The Brain

03

The Scale Layer

Guided onboarding, pricing, integrations, templates, support AI, benchmark automation, and pilot evidence tracking keep growth from requiring more staff per customer.

Validation moat

The app must prove the signal predicts real outcomes.

The biggest investor risk is not whether the interface looks smart. It is whether Cognitive DNA predicts job performance. Auctor should include a pilot evidence dashboard from day one.

Outcome tracking

Compare Auctor scores with 30, 60, and 90-day supervisor ratings, task completion, safety events, and retention.

Fairness monitoring

Track adverse impact, consent status, accessibility accommodations, and confidence intervals by cohort.

Benchmark network

Build anonymized industry benchmarks that improve defensibility without exposing customer data.

Investor-ready MVP direction

Start narrow, then expand into the standard for potential.

The first version should focus on one industry, one physical challenge pack, one role intelligence builder, one employer dashboard, and one validation study. This prototype shows the broader vision while keeping the MVP path credible.
